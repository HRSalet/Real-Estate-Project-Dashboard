# Real Estate Dashboard

## 1. Overview

The Real Estate Dashboard is a dynamic and an interactive visually rich tool that combines essential data and elements concerning real estate management. The dashboard offers a comprehensive overview of real estate project outcomes, allowing users to analyze revenue, expenses, profits and property sales.

## 2. Purpose

The Real Estate Dashboard is visually engaging and analytical Power BI report aims to provide a clear and short summary of the project's financial health and sales outcomes. The dashboard is designed for straightforward navigation and easily understanding of complex data through intuitive visualizations.

## 3. Tech Stack<br>

The dashboard was built using the following tools and technologies:
- 📊 Power BI Desktop – Main data visualization platform used for report creation.
- 📂 Power Query - Data Transformation and cleaning layer for reshaping and preparing the data.
- 🧠 DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) – Used for calculated measures, and dynamic visuals.
- 📝 Data Modeling - Relationships established among tables (customer details, property details, and calender) to enable cross-filtering and aggregation.
- 📁 File Format – .pbix for development and .png for dashboard previews.

## 4. Key Metrics

At the bottom of the dashboard are four essential key performance indicators (KPIs) that provide a quick overview of the project's status:

- **Revenue 26 Million(M):** This metric reflects overall revenue generated from property sales and other related activities within the real estate project. A substantial revenue amount signals excellent sales result and market interest in the properties.

- **Expenses 20 Million(M):** Expenses consists all expenditures associated with real estate project, including marketing, maintenance, operational costs, and variuos overheads. Tracking expenses is essential for managing costs and maintaining profitability.

- **Profits 6 Million(M):** Profit is calculated by subtracting total expenses from total revenue. It represents the profit or loss from the project. A positive profit margin, as demonstrated here, shows a successful and financially sustainable project.

- **Sold Properties 26:** This measure calculates the overall amount of properties successfully sold during the reporting period. It serves as a clear measure of sales volume and market presence.

These KPIs are displayed to give users instant insights into the project's overall performance, serving as a quick reference for stakeholders and decision-makers.

## 5. Visualizations

The dashboard utilizes different types of visualizations to represent data in an easily understable format, allows for deeper insights into the real estate project.

**1. Revenue by Country (Map Visualization)**<br>
This visualization display the geographical distribution of revenue. Countries are coloured based on revenue produced, with darker color usually representing greater revenue contributions. For example, Chile and New Zealand shows maximum revenue contributions, indicates robust market presence or effective sales in these region.

**2. Revenue by Country (Table)**<br>
This visualization represent revenue of different countries in a table format, which offers a detailed numerical analysis of revenue by country. It displays every country with its associated revenue figure, enabling understandable analysis and comparison. This table format is specially helpful for displaying precise revenue amounts that may be not properly visible on the map.

**3. Real Estate Agent Performance (Table)**<br>
This table offers an overview of the achievements of specific real estate agents. It includes a picture of every agent, along with their name and the income they have produced. This visual representation is essential for evaluating personal contributions, recognizing the top performers. The combined revenue produced by these agents are upto 26M, matching the overall revenue KPI.

**4. Glance of Properties (Carousel Slider)**<br>
This segment acts as a visual display of the properties included in the real estate project. Though it isn't a proper data visualization, it offers crucial context and a concrete representation of the assets that are managed and sold. Showing images of properties helps in connecting the abstract financial data with physical aspects of the real estate business, enhance the dashboard's relevance and appeal for users.

**5. Quarter-Wise Expense (Area Chart)**<br>
This area chart illustrates the pattern of expenses throughout four quarters. It assists to understand spending trends over the year and identifying periods of high and low spending. The data points are:
- Qtr 1: 1.9M
- Qtr 2: 8.6M
- Qtr 3: 4.7M
- Qtr 4: 4.6M

The chart clearly shows a significant increment in expenses in Qtr 2, succeeded by a decrement in Qtr 3 and Qtr 4. This may suggest seasonsal operational expenses, large-scale marketing, or distinct project phases that resulted in increased expenses.

**6. Revenue by Month Name (Bar Chart)**<br>
This bar chart depicts the monthly revenue trends, offering a detailed view on sales performance across the year. analyzing this chart can reveal seasonal trends in the real estate market, the effects of particular marketing efforts, or economic variables affecting sales. The bars shows the revenue for each month from February to December, providing a clear visual comparison of monthly performace.

**7. Revenue by Country (Pareto Chart)**<br>
This Pareto chart combines a bar chart with a line chart to deliver an effective analysis of revenue by country. The bars represent the revenue produced by each country, arranged from highest to lowest, while the line graph depicts the cumulative total revenue percentage. This chart particularly useful for applying 80/20 rule (Pareto Principle), indentifying the select countries that contributes the majority of the total revenue. The countries included are Chile, New Zealand, Egypt, Nigeria, United States, and Spain. This visualization helps in focusing efforts and resources on the areas with the greatest impact.

## 6. Filters

The dashboard fetaures interactive components to improve user experience and data analysis. The presence of year filters (2025, 2026, 2027) indicates that users can flexibly modify data shown to concentrate on particular intervals. This feature is essential for analyzing history, recognizing trends, and planning for the future.
